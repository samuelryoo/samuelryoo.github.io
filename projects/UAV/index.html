Multirotor UAV Avionics & Embedded Systems

This project involved the integration and testing of a multirotor UAV avionics system, including flight controllers, ESCs, radio links, GPS, telemetry, and onboard power distribution. The primary objective was to configure a reliable embedded flight control stack capable of stable operation under real-world electrical, mechanical, and environmental constraints.

Work focused on configuring and validating flight control firmware, integrating PWM-driven motor control with UART-based peripherals, and ensuring correct interaction between navigation, telemetry, and radio subsystems. Particular attention was given to failsafe behavior, signal integrity, and control responsiveness across multiple flight modes.

Key engineering challenges included managing power and signal routing, tuning control parameters for stability, and diagnosing system-level issues observed during ground and flight testing. These challenges were addressed through iterative configuration, telemetry review, and controlled flight tests to verify system behavior. The resulting platform demonstrates stable flight performance and provides a solid foundation for deeper analysis of flight dynamics, control tuning, and embedded avionics development.
