Cadence Virtuoso CMOS Device Sizing Timing Analysis

Transistor-Level NAND Gate Design & Characterization

This project focused on the optimization of a CMOS NAND gate at the transistor level using Cadence Virtuoso. The objective was to design a functionally correct logic gate and to systematically optimize device sizing to achieve balanced switching behavior and predictable timing under realistic operating conditions.

The design process involved implementing PMOS pull-up and NMOS pull-down networks and iteratively tuning transistor dimensions based on voltage transfer characteristics and transient waveform analysis. Special attention was given to centering the switching threshold near mid-supply and minimizing rise/fall asymmetry caused by carrier mobility differences.

Timing performance was characterized through parametric sweeps of supply voltage and temperature to evaluate delay sensitivity and robustness. These experiments highlighted speed–stability trade-offs and the impact of environmental variation on propagation delay. The final design exhibits stable logic behavior and serves as a well-characterized baseline for extending optimization techniques to more complex CMOS circuits.
