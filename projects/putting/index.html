Putting Start-Line & Speed Measurement System

Low-profile optical measurement system for estimating ball launch angle and initial speed during putting.

Embedded Sensing Timing & Measurement ESP32 / Arduino

This project implements a low-profile optical measurement system designed to quantify two critical parameters of a putting stroke: initial start line and launch speed. The goal was to provide immediate, objective feedback without relying on club-mounted sensors or altering ball roll characteristics.

The system uses two infrared sensing gates positioned at fixed distances in front of the ball. Multiple break-beam sensors within each gate detect lateral crossing order and timestamp ball passage. Launch angle is estimated from left/right crossing order, while time-of-flight between gates is used to compute initial ball speed. An embedded microcontroller timestamps events at kilohertz-scale resolution and evaluates results against configurable tolerances.

Design constraints emphasized deterministic timing, low latency feedback, and ease of use. The hardware was kept under 25 mm in height to avoid influencing roll, while firmware was structured to deliver feedback within sub-200 ms end-to-end latency. Verification included angle-board testing, ramp-based speed validation, and repeated setup trials to confirm accuracy, responsiveness, and usability. The resulting system demonstrates repeatable measurement performance and a firmware architecture suitable for extension to additional metrics or wireless data logging.
