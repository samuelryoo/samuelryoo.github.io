Robot Arm

Multi-DOF arm with servo control; kinematics, timing, and embedded control loops.

Embedded C/C++ Control

Designed and built a multi-degree-of-freedom robotic arm driven by servos/steppers and an embedded controller. I focused on motion reliability and repeatability: tuning PWM, shaping motion profiles, and organizing the control loop so inputs, safety checks, and actuation stayed responsive under load.

My work covered firmware for joint control, timing/jitter mitigation, and integrating drivers, power, and mechanics. Key challenges included torque vs. speed trade-offs, heat/current limits, and smoothing abrupt commands so the arm moved naturally without overshoot.

The result is a stable demo that executes gestures consistently, with a documented setup that makes it easy to add trajectories, limit switches, or higher-level kinematics later.
