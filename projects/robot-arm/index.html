Multi-DOF Robotic Arm Control System

This project involved the design and implementation of a multi-degree-of-freedom robotic arm driven by servo and stepper actuators and controlled by an embedded system. The primary objective was to achieve reliable and repeatable motion while maintaining responsive control under varying mechanical loads and power constraints.

The control firmware was structured to manage joint-level actuation through carefully tuned PWM signals and stepper timing, with a focus on minimizing jitter and ensuring deterministic behavior. Motion commands, safety checks, and actuator updates were organized within a responsive control loop to prevent blocking behavior and maintain stable operation during continuous motion.

Key engineering challenges included balancing torque and speed trade-offs, managing current and thermal limits, and shaping motion profiles to reduce abrupt transitions and overshoot. These issues were addressed through incremental tuning of control parameters and structured sequencing of actuator commands. The resulting system demonstrates consistent, repeatable motion and provides a modular foundation for future extensions such as trajectory planning, limit switches, or higher-level kinematic control.
